## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Short to medium. 8-18 words typical. Uses fragments for emphasis: "No hidden fees." "No complexity." Rarely goes over 25 words. When it does, it's a stat-loaded proof sentence.

**Paragraphs:** 1-3 sentences max. Lots of breathing room. One idea per paragraph. Never walls of text.

**Openings:** Lead with the outcome or the pain point, never the feature.
- Outcome: "Gym owners using a Kilo website report getting 18% more leads each month."
- Pain: "Leads slip through the cracks."
- Identity: "Software Built to Grow Coaching Gyms."

**Formatting:**
- Bold for emphasis on key stats and claims
- Tables for comparison (pain/solution, us/competitor)
- Bulleted lists for features, kept tight
- Headers use sentence case or title case, short and scannable
- CTAs repeated multiple times per page â€” not pushy, just always available
- Stats displayed as large numbers with supporting context

**Closing pattern:** End with a clear, simple CTA. "Watch a demo" or "Book a free call." Often paired with a reassurance: "all for just $375/month" or "No hidden fees."
